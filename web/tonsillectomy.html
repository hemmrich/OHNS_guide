Indications

- Obstructive sleep apnea - Recurrent tonsillitis (Paradise criteria: 7 infections in 1 year, 5 infections per year for 2 consecutive years, 3 infections per year for 3 consecutive years)

Procedure Overview

1) Expose oropharynx using Crowe-Davis retractor 2) Pass red rubber catheter into nare and out through the mouth to retract soft palate 3) Clamp body of tonsil with Allis and pull to medialize 4) Use monopolar cautery to dissect through mucosa at superior pole until fibrous capsule is reached 5) etc... Notes: - Hot technique using electrocautery preferred compared to cold steel technique - Adenoidectomy performed using either suction cautery or adenoid curette

Complications

- Hemorrhage (primary if within 24 hours, secondary if after 24 hours)
