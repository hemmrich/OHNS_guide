Indications

  • Neck dissections (ND) involve the removal of lymph nodes from the neck
  • Lymph node levels removed depends on site of primary tumor and predicted spread
  • Staging ND: used to determine need for post-op chemo or radiation
  • Therapeutic ND: for patients at high risk of regional metastasis
  • Radical ND: additional resection of IJV, CN XI, or SCM

Procedure overview

  • Incision is made and subplatysmal flaps are raised
  • ADD MORE STEPS

Complications

  • Common to all surgeries: bleeding/hematoma, infection
  • Chyle leaks, especially in NDs involving level IV
  • Damage to marginal mandibular nerve: impaired lower lip movement
  • Damage to CN XI: impaired SCM and trapezius movement
  • Damage to hypoglossal, lingual, and vagus nerves is rare

Neck dissection levels

  • Level Ia (submental triangle): between anterior bellies of diastric and hyoid
  • Level Ib (submandibular triangle): between anterior and posterior belly of digastric and mandible
  • Level II (upper jugular nodes): stylohyoid to posterior border of SCM, skull base to hyoid
  • Level IIb (posterior upper jugular nodes):
  • Level III (middle jugular nodes)
  • Level IV (lower jugular nodes)
  • Level Va (superior posterior triangle)
  • Level Vb (inferior posterior triangle)
  • Level VI (anterior compartment)
