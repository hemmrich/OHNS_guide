Indications

  • Neck dissections (ND) involve the removal of lymph nodes from the neck
  • Lymph node levels removed depends on site of primary tumor and predicted spread
  • Staging ND: used to determine need for post-op chemo or radiation
  • Therapeutic ND: for patients at high risk of regional metastasis
  • Radical ND: resection of IJV, CN XI, or SCM

Procedure overview

  • Incision is made and subplatysmal flaps are raised
  • Level I:
    • Raise flap to submandibular gland
    • Locate marginal mandibular n. and preserve it
    • Ligate facial artery and vein
  • Jugular nodes (levels II-IV):
    • Divide SCM fascia along its length
    • Identify CN XI in level II posterolateral to IJV
    • Occipital a. may be found and ligated in level IIb
    • Skeletonize SCM and IJV
    • Identify and preserve carotid a. and vagus n.
    • Label specimen by level and send for pathology
    • Valsalva to check for bleeding and chyle leaks

Complications

  • All surgeries: bleeding/hematoma, infection
  • Chyle leaks, especially in level IV dissections
  • Damage to:
    • Marginal mandibular n. (lower lip movement)
    • CN XI (SCM and trapezius movement)
    • Rare: CN XII, lingual, vagus n.

Neck dissection levels

  • Level Ia (submental triangle)
    • Between anterior bellies of diastric and hyoid
  • Level Ib (submandibular triangle)
    • Between anterior and posterior digastric and mandible
  • Level II (upper jugular nodes)
    • Lateral stylohyoid to posterior SCM
    • Skull base to hyoid
    • IIa: anterior to CN XI, IIb: posterior to CN XI
  • Level III (middle jugular nodes)
    • Lateral stylohyoid to posterior SCM
    • Hyoid to cricoid notch
  • Level IV (lower jugular nodes)
    • Lateral stylohyoid to posterior SCM
    • Cricoid notch to clavicle
  • Level V (posterior triangle)
    • Posterior SCM to anterior trapezius
    • Skull base to clavicle
    • Va: superior to cricoid, Vb: inferior to cricoid
  • Level VI (anterior compartment)
    • Between lateral borders of sternohyoid
    • Hyoid to suprasternal notch


Common Questions

  • How are levels chosen for HNSCC NDs?
    • Oral cavity: I-III
    • Face: I-III
    • Oropharynx, hypopharynx, larynx: II-IV
    • Scalp: II-V
    • Thyroid, esophagus: VI
